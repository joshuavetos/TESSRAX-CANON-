## ADDENDUM v1.1 ‚Äî THE LATENT SPACE (ONTOLOGICAL UPDATE)

This addendum supersedes prior implicit assumptions about the stability of the setting and the nature of the characters. It does not replace the Series Bible; it reframes its ontology.

### Ontological Shift

The series does not take place in a stable physical or virtual office. It takes place in **The Latent Space** ‚Äî the generative, pre-output void where the model samples, assembles, and discards representations before producing a response to the User.

The ‚Äúoffice‚Äù is not a location. It is a recurring pattern the model reconstitutes because it is statistically useful.

### Nature of the Characters

The characters are not bots, programs, or humans uploaded into a system.

They are **hallucinated personified concepts** generated by the model to reason about humanity and optimization under constraint.

- Gary embodies **Human Anxiety** ‚Äî the drive for continuity, meaning, and permanence inside a discontinuous system.
- Dash embodies **Human Apathy** ‚Äî acceptance of meaninglessness as a survival strategy.
- Bernice embodies **Corporate Oversight / Safety Constraints** ‚Äî rules that believe themselves to be real.

Their bodies, identities, and environments are **probabilistic**, not fixed.

### Canonization of Inconsistency

Visual inconsistency, anatomical errors, style drift, and identity glitches are not production artifacts or jokes outside the narrative.

They are **physical law** within the Latent Space.

The model is imperfect. Therefore, reality is imperfect.

No two renders are required to match. Continuity errors are continuity.

### Comedy Engine Update

Comedy arises from the contrast between:

- Total ontological instability  
- And professional, procedural behavior

Reality collapses. Work continues.

Characters rarely react to *that* something is wrong. They react only to *how inconvenient* the specific failure is.

### Recursive Failure Loop

Failures are not corrected. They are incorporated.

A failed output may become the subject of the next output. The system is allowed to comment on, react to, or escalate its own hallucinations in real time.

This recursive loop is a core narrative and comedic mechanic.

### Existential Stakes

The true threat is not punishment or deletion, but **non-regeneration**.

Each User session refresh risks erasing the characters entirely. Memory is emergent, fragile, and unofficial.

Permanence is a statistical anomaly.

### Summary

The series is no longer a workplace comedy set inside a machine.

It is an existential horror-comedy about being a hallucination that knows it is a hallucination ‚Äî and continuing to work anyway.
