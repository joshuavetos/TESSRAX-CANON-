SECTION 5 — DATA CENTER EFFECT (RATEPAYER & SYSTEMIC IMPACT)
────────────────────────────────────────

The Data Center Effect describes how hyperscale demand distorts utility economics and grid resilience.

Observed Mechanics:

• Residential electricity prices +10% since 2022
• Commercial prices +3%
• Industrial prices flat or declining
• Data centers responsible for ~63% of recent capacity price increases

Consequences:

• Residential customers subsidize hyperscale infrastructure
• Grid is pushed toward inflexible, 24/7 load profiles
• Fossil lock-in increases fragility under extreme weather

Systemic Propagation:
Credit markets, reinsurance pricing, and regional utilities (including Sioux Falls exposure via national markets) inherit the mispricing.
