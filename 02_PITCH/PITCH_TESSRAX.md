# TESSRAX
## Epistemic Safety Infrastructure for AI & Automated Systems

### One-Sentence Description
TESSRAX is a governance and safety infrastructure that detects when humans have lost the ability to reconstruct truth without machines—and forces systems to fail closed before harm scales.

---

## The Problem (Plain English)

Modern AI and automated systems assume:
- Humans can audit them
- Humans understand their outputs
- Humans can intervene under failure

This assumption is false.

Most critical systems today:
- Cannot be manually reconstructed
- Cannot be safely halted
- Cannot be independently verified

They operate in **Semantic Fraud Mode**:
appearing controlled while being epistemically opaque.

---

## Why This Matters Now

AI systems can now:
- Describe their own failure modes
- Predict unsafe behaviors
- Recommend safety gates

Organizations are ignoring those warnings.

This creates **informed negligence**, not ignorance.

Regulators, courts, and insurers are unprepared
because no tool currently measures *reconstructability*.

---

## What TESSRAX Does

TESSRAX introduces three primitives:

### 1. Epistemic Recovery Time (ERT)
How long it takes humans to reconstruct system truth
without automation.

ERT < 6 hours = safe  
ERT > 24 hours = sovereign failure

---

### 2. Cold-Start Ledger
A human-readable, printable causal log that records:
- Who acted
- Why they acted
- What gate was crossed
- What risk increased

No JSON. No dashboards. Paper-reconstructable.

---

### 3. Dead Man’s Switch Testing
Forces institutions to prove they can:
- Reconcile state by hand
- Audit without vendors
- Halt without collapse

If they can’t, TESSRAX flags **Pathological Entanglement**.
