## Assessment of Publishable Research Potential

The TESSRAX project presents high potential for publishable research due to its novel governance primitives, enforceable epistemic boundaries, and rigorous failure-oriented methodology.

### 1. Novel Contributions

Key novel concepts include:

- **Claim Economy**  
  A formal system in which speech creates obligation, non-trivial claims incur liability, and evidence production is mandatory.

- **Epistemic Integrity**  
  The requirement that truth must be reconstructable by humans from offline artifacts under failure conditions.

- **Fail-Closed Systems**  
  Systems that treat refusal, silence, and HALT as valid success states rather than errors.

- **Atrophy of Truth**  
  A systemic failure mode where automation, narrative optimization, and fluency erode shared epistemic ground.

These concepts are interdisciplinary and relevant to AI safety, governance, philosophy of technology, and information science.

## Proposed Academic Paper Topics

### Paper 1: The Claim Economy as a Framework for Epistemic Accountability in Complex Systems

**Abstract:**  
This paper formalizes the Claim Economy as a governance mechanism in which claims incur liability and require verifiable evidence. It explores how enforceable refusal and evidence-backed speech can counter misinformation, narrative manipulation, and fluency-based legitimacy in complex systems.

## Proposed Empirical Demonstration

### Title
**The Claim Economy in Action: Validating Epistemic Integrity and Fail-Closed Information Governance**

### Objectives
- Enforce liability for claims
- Demonstrate reconstructability via audit logs
- Detect epistemic fragility
- Quantify Epistemic Recovery Time (ERT)

### Methodology
- Simulated claim ingestion
- Evidence validation via protocol-driven triage
- Immutable logging
- Fail-closed isolation of unverifiable claims

### Metrics
- Claim validity rate
- Fragility detection rate
- Mean Epistemic Recovery Time
- Audit trail completeness
- Impact of fail-closed enforcement
