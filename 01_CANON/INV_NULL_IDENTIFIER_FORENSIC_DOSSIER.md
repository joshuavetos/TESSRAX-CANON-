## 1. The Core Theorem

### The "Null Identifier" (Definition)
A governance mechanism where a long-dated target year (e.g., “2050”) functions not as a deadline for delivery, but as a container for liability. It allows institutions to import legitimacy from the future to subsidize extraction in the present, while formally suspending the requirement for funding or operational specificity.

### The "Horizon Offset" (Mechanism)
The systematic decoupling of the Vision Horizon (20–30 years, binding public trust) from the Fiscal Horizon (1–5 years, binding capital allocation). The fraud occurs in the temporal gap between the two, where “plans” exist without “funding.”

### Mathematical Formula for Institutional Fraud
As the temporal gap increases, the requirement for funded capital allocation approaches zero.
