CONCEPT: Pre-Incident Institutional Forensics

Core Insight:
There is a distinct, under-served space for pre-incident, record-binding contradiction detection inside institutions. This work is neither consulting nor post-incident compliance. Its leverage comes from producing formal artifacts that force an institution to Fix, Delay, or Explicitly Accept Risk on the record.

Primary Value:
Power comes from artifacts, not advice. The goal is to eliminate plausible deniability by timestamping documented contradictions between stated policy and operational reality.

Target Audience:
General Counsel, Risk Officers, Boards, Insurers.
(Not product teams, not strategy, not engineering velocity roles.)

Minimum Viable Artifact (MVA):
A one-page, board-attachable document that records a structural inconsistency and requires a disposition.

Artifact Types (working names):
- Notice of Structural Inconsistency (NSI)
- Gap Notice
- Contradiction Certificate
- Authority Mismatch Log

Core Requirement:
Each artifact must force a binary institutional response:
☐ Fix
☐ Delay
☐ Accept Risk (explicitly, on the record)

Key Constraint:
No persuasion, no recommendations, no narrative framing. Only documented deltas between documents, authority, and implementation.
