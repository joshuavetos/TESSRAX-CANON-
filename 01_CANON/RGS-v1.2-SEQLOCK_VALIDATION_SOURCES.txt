RGS-v1.2-SEQLOCK — VALIDATION SOURCES & PRECEDENT MAP
Status: CANONICAL SUPPORTING EVIDENCE

Method: Source-anchored mapping only. No interpretive narrative, no forward claims.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 1 — FAIL-CLOSED & DETERMINISTIC ENFORCEMENT
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
	1.	NIST SP 800-53 Rev. 5
Title: Security and Privacy Controls for Information Systems and Organizations
Relevant Controls:

   •   SI-7 (Integrity) — mandates detection and response to integrity failures
   •   SR-3 / SR-5 (Supply Chain Risk) — non-bypassable control enforcement
Relevance: Establishes fail-closed behavior as a baseline requirement when integrity cannot be proven.

	2.	DO-178C (RTCA)
Title: Software Considerations in Airborne Systems and Equipment Certification
Relevance:

   •   Deterministic execution paths
   •   Static verification over runtime discretion
   •   Explicit rejection of ambiguous or unverifiable behavior
Closest real-world analogue to RGS static gate evaluation.

	3.	ISO/IEC 15408 (Common Criteria)
Relevance:

   •   Evaluation against a fixed Target of Evaluation (TOE)
   •   Assurance levels based on proof, not intent
Supports claim grounding via canonical artifacts rather than narrative claims.

SECTION 2 — REPLAYABILITY & CONTEXT HASHING
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
	4.	Reproducible Builds (Debian, NixOS)

   •   Basis for ledger integrity and replay verification.

Relevance:

   •   External verifiability of non-removal and non-rewriting
