## INTERVIEW ANSWER PACK

### How do you prioritize competing requests?
I start by identifying the dominant constraint. Often two requests feel equally urgent, but one has a hard technical, regulatory, or operational cutoff while the other is more flexible. I align stakeholders by showing how that hard constraint dictates sequencing, so the system stays stable instead of reactive.

### Tell me about a time you handled conflict between teams.
Most conflict comes from teams working off different facts. I’ve resolved deadlocks by shifting the discussion away from personal preferences and onto shared constraints and success criteria. Once everyone is evaluating options against the same requirements, the right path usually becomes clear without escalation.

### How do you handle scope creep or extra requests mid-project?
I’m transparent about the cost of yes. I don’t reject ideas outright—I show the trade-off. We can add the request, but it will shift the timeline or require reallocating resources. Framing it as a choice between outcomes allows leadership to make an informed decision while protecting the original commitment.

## RED-FLAG DETECTOR (QUESTIONS YOU ASK)

- When two departments have conflicting priorities for the same resource, how is the tie-breaker decided?
- What is the process for saying “no” or “not now” once a project is already in motion?
- How does the team handle missed deadlines caused by systemic issues rather than individual mistakes?
- If I asked three different people what the top priority is this quarter, would I get the same answer?

Failure on these questions indicates high entropy, hero culture, or unbounded scope.
